## §15. Glossary

Brief explanations of technical terms used throughout this report.

**Antardasha (AD)** — sub-period within a Mahadasha. Vimshottari system: 9 Antardashas per Mahadasha, each proportional to the relative Vimshottari-weights of the two planets involved.

**Arudha Lagna (AL)** — "projected image" of the self, computed as the lord's placement-distance-from-Lagna doubled and marked forward. Classical reading: how one appears to the world as distinct from what one actually is.

**Ashtakavarga (AV)** — bindu-system where each planet contributes 1-8 points to each sign based on its placement relative to other planets. Sarvashtakavarga (SAV) = total per sign; Bhinnashtakavarga (BAV) = per-planet contribution. Threshold interpretation: SAV >30 strong, ~28 average, <26 weak.

**Atmakaraka (AK)** — "soul significator." The planet with the highest degree (irrespective of sign) in the D1 chart per Jaimini tradition. Your AK is Moon at 27°02'48".

**Bhavabala (BVB)** — house-strength metric combining lord-strength, Dig Bala, aspect-load, Bhav Madhya position, and other factors. Classical thresholds: >7 Rupas strong; 5-7 Rupas adequate; <5 Rupas structurally weak.

**Bhrigu Bindu** — destiny-focus-point computed as the midpoint of Moon and Rahu longitudes. Classical significance: where karmic attention naturally crystallizes in the lifetime.

**Chalit chart** — house-system based on degree-cusps (Sripathi bhava). Differs from Rashi-chart where each sign equals one house. Used for transit-trigger timing and outcome-delivery reading; Rashi is used for yoga-formation and aspect analysis.

**Chara Dasha** — Jaimini sign-based dasha system where each sign becomes the Mahadasha lord in sequence. K.N. Rao Padakrama variant is used in this report.

**Combustion (CMB)** — planet within specified orb of the Sun. Orbs vary by planet (Mercury 12°, Venus 10°, Mars 17°, Jupiter 11°, Saturn 15°). Combust planets deliver with restricted visibility.

**CYSS (Composite Yoga Signal Score)** — analytical framework score normalizing yoga-strength to 0-100. Integrates constituent planetary Shadbala, Vimsopaka, house-Bhavabala, Ashtakavarga, and specific dignity-bonuses. Scores >90 are peak-amplitude yogas; 70-90 are operational; <70 are technically-present but not strongly-delivering.

**DAS (Dasha Activation Score)** — analytical framework score combining eight activation factors to produce a per-month 0-100 activation-level assessment. Tier thresholds: dormant <40, baseline 40-59, elevated 60-74, peak 75-89, critical 90-100.

**Dig Bala (DIG)** — directional strength. Each planet has a specific house where its Dig Bala is maximum (60) — Sun in 10H, Moon in 4H, Mars in 10H, Mercury in 1H, Jupiter in 1H, Venus in 4H, Saturn in 7H.

**D1, D9, D10, etc.** — Rashi (D1), Navamsa (D9), Dashamsha (D10), and other divisional charts. Each divisional divides each sign into equal divisions and re-assigns the planets. Classical tradition uses D1 for general, D9 for dharma-marriage, D10 for career, D12 for family-ancestry, etc.

**Exaltation / Debilitation** — specific signs where each planet is classically considered strongest (exalted) or weakest (debilitated). Exaltation signs: Sun-Aries, Moon-Taurus, Mars-Capricorn, Mercury-Virgo, Jupiter-Cancer, Venus-Pisces, Saturn-Libra. Debilitation signs are 180° opposite each exaltation.

**Ishta Devata** — chosen deity for direct sustained worship. Classical derivation: 12th from AK in D9. Your Ishta: Mahalakshmi.

**Karakamsa** — the sign/house in the D9 chart that contains the AK. Classical reading: the sign where the soul-purpose manifests in the dharma-chart. Your Karakamsa: Gemini (D9 12H).

**Mahadasha (MD)** — primary planetary-period in Vimshottari Dasha. Nine sequential periods, each proportional to a specific Vimshottari-weight (Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rahu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17, Ketu 7, Venus 20 years). Total cycle 120 years.

**Muntha** — Tajika annual focus point, advancing one sign per year from natal Lagna. Indicates where the year's karmic attention concentrates.

**Nakshatra** — one of 27 (or sometimes 28) lunar mansions, each spanning 13°20' of the zodiac. Each nakshatra is divided into 4 padas (quarters).

**Neecha Bhanga Raja Yoga (NBRY)** — "cancellation of debilitation" yoga. Debilitated planet's weakness is cancelled by a specific cancellation condition (BPHS Ch.37), producing delayed-but-unusually-high-peak delivery in the planet's significations.

**Panchang** — five-limb calendar system specifying daily Tithi (lunar day), Nakshatra, Yoga, Karana, and Vara (weekday). Used for muhurta (auspicious timing) selection.

**Pratyantardasha (PD)** — sub-sub-period within an Antardasha. Typically 2-4 months in duration.

**Purva Bhadrapada (PB)** — the 25th nakshatra, spanning Aquarius 20° to Pisces 3°20'. Ruled by Jupiter. Classified as Ugra (fierce) category.

**Rashi** — zodiac sign (Aries through Pisces). Each sign is 30° of the zodiac.

**Ratnashastra** — classical Indian gemstone-science tradition. Primary texts include Narahari's Rajanighantu, Mani-Mala, and regional Ratnashastra compendiums.

**Shadbala** — six-fold strength measurement producing a composite planetary-strength score. Components: Sthana Bala (positional), Dig Bala (directional), Kaala Bala (temporal), Chesta Bala (motional), Naisargika Bala (natural), Drik Bala (aspectual). Total expressed in Virupa (units of 1/60 of a degree) or Rupas (units of a degree).

**Shuddha Pinda** — refined Ashtakavarga-bindu score after reduction per classical methods. Measures "karmic permission field" for each planet.

**Uccha Bala** — exaltation-proximity strength. One of the six Shadbala components. Measures how close a planet is to its exaltation degree.

**Varshphal** — Tajika annual chart cast for the moment of solar return each year. Year-specific overlay on the natal chart.

**Vimshottari Dasha** — Parashari dasha system producing sequential Mahadashas totaling 120 years. Most widely-used dasha system in North Indian astrological tradition.

**Vimsopaka Bala** — divisional-chart strength measurement across 16 vargas (divisional charts). Measures dispersion of planetary strength across dharma-dimensional levels.

**Yogini Dasha** — 36-year cyclical dasha system with 8 Mahadashas (Mangala, Pingala, Dhanya, Bhramari, Bhadrika, Ulka, Siddha, Sankata). Used as temporal-overlay alongside Vimshottari.
