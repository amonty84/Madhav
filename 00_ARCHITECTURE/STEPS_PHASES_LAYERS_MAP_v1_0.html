Three orthogonal axes

The project runs on three separate indexing systems that mean different things. The Macro Plan references all three without flagging that they're different in kind — and that's the cognitive cost this map exists to remove.

Layers (L1 → L6+) — the architecture

This is where knowledge lives, ordered by derivation depth. L1 is the raw fact base — the chart computations and the biographical timeline. L2 is atomized signals (the Master Signal Register, 499 entries). L2.5 is holistic synthesis across signals (UCN, CDLM, CGM, RM). L3 is consumer-facing domain reports. L4 is the Discovery Layer where Gemini surfaces patterns. L5/L6 is the Learning Layer substrate plus temporal and probabilistic layers. Layers are static architecture — they describe the shape of the corpus, not what's happening at any moment.

Phases (M1 → M10) — the arc

This is what the project does over time. Each phase builds layers, activates them, or extends them. M1 built L1 + L2 + L2.5 + L3 and is empirically closed as of 2026-04-19. M2 activates L2.5 via graph-RAG and builds L4 while scaffolding L5/L6 — it's the current phase, currently paused pending the governance rebuild. M3–M10 progressively add temporal computations, calibration, probabilistic modelling, prospective testing, cohort extension, classical cross-reference, multi-school triangulation, and the final acharya-facing interface. Phases are chronological — only one is active at a time.

Steps (0 → 15) — the governance rebuild

This is a one-off meta-task, not part of the macro arc at all. It pauses M2 to rebuild the project's governance infrastructure — the ledgers, registries, protocols, drift-detectors, and templates that keep multi-session work coherent. Steps produce governance artifacts; they do not touch L1, L2, L2.5, or any layer content. We're at Step 2 right now; the step will close once the native signs off on the revision spec, then Steps 3 through 15 produce the rewritten Macro Plan, the Integrity Protocol, the rebuilt CLAUDE.md, and finally the GOVERNANCE_BASELINE_v1_0.md that unfreezes M2.

How they connect

The current Step (Step 2) writes a spec about how the new Macro Plan will describe the Phases and Layers. Step 3 produces MACRO_PLAN_v2_0.md, which is the document that names the phases and points to the layers. After Step 15, M2 phase work resumes at B.0 of the PHASE_B_PLAN — and that work is what actually builds L4 Discovery and scaffolds the Learning Layer. So Step 2 → Step 15 is governance housekeeping; then the project returns to M2 corpus-activation, which is phase-work that builds layers.
