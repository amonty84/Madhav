### §27 — Glossary

**AK (Atmakaraka)**: The planet with the highest longitudinal degree (sign-stripped) among the seven non-nodal planets. = Moon (27°02'). Represents the soul's primary significator and the planet most loaded with the native's karmic curriculum.

**Avastha**: The state or condition of a planet at birth. Susupta = deep sleep (profoundly internalized operation). Mrat = death-age (post-cycle wisdom state). Shant = peaceful mood (underlying equanimity). Both Mercury and Moon share this Avastha configuration.

**Chandra Lagna**: The "from-Moon" chart, where the Moon's house is treated as the 1H and all houses are re-counted from there. Provides insight into the native's internal psychological orientation vs. the external D1 Lagna orientation.

**DK (Darakaraka)**: The planet with the lowest longitudinal degree (sign-stripped) among the seven non-nodal planets. = Mercury (00°50'). Represents the spouse and primary relational partners by Jaimini system.

**Karakamsa**: The sign that the AK (Moon) occupies in the D9 Navamsa. = Gemini (Mercury's sign). Represents the soul's highest expression domain.

**Purva Bhadrapada**: The 25th nakshatra (Aquarius 20° – Pisces 3°20'). Ruled by Jupiter. Deity: Aja Ekapada. Classical character: fierce (Ugra), intensely transformational, philosophically profound, capable of extreme sacrifice for principles. Moon's natal nakshatra.

**Susupta**: The "deep sleep" state in the Jagratadi (consciousness) Avastha system. Indicates the planet operates from a deeply internalized, below-surface mode. Both Moon and Mercury in this state = the mind's power is internal and deep, not externally volatile.

**Three-Layer Mind Architecture**: This report's organizing framework. Layer 1 = Aries Lagna (Mars-drive), Layer 2 = Moon AK Aquarius (soul-emotion), Layer 3 = Mercury Capricorn (analytical instrument). Integration of these three layers is the psychological curriculum of this lifetime.

**Uttara Ashadha**: The 21st nakshatra. Ruled by Sun. Classical character: persistent, ethical, patient, universally applicable, successful through sustained long-term effort. Mercury's natal nakshatra.

**Vargottama**: The condition where a planet occupies the same sign in both D1 (Rashi) and D9 (Navamsa). = Mercury. The highest single-attribute dignity confirmation in Jyotish — indicates maximum consistency across life domains.
