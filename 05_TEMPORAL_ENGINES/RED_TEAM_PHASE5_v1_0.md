## §2 — REMEDIAL CODEX AUDIT

### §2.1 — Gem recommendations (emerald, yellow sapphire) — coherence with chart

- **Emerald for Mercury**: Chart-aligned. Mercury is MD lord + 7-system + Yogi + Karakamsa lord. Emerald activates Mercury; given Mercury's architectural centrality, this is the highest-leverage gem recommendation. APPROVED.
- **Yellow Sapphire for Jupiter**: Chart-aligned. Jupiter is 9L own-sign own-sign dharma lord at max dignity. Yellow Sapphire reinforces dharmic authority. APPROVED.
- **Blue Sapphire avoided for Saturn**: Chart-aligned rationale. Saturn is AmK + exalted + AD lord; already delivering at max capacity. Additional amplification via Blue Sapphire would overload Saturn's structural weight. APPROVED avoidance.
- **Ruby avoided for Sun**: Sun in enemy sign (Capricorn for Sun). Ruby's raw solar amplification conflicts with the Saturnian-structured Sun expression. APPROVED avoidance.

### §2.2 — "Don't remediate enemies" principle

The Codex's core principle — that classical "Saturn pacification" and "Mars pacification" misread this chart — is architecturally correct. Saturn here is exalted AmK (beneficial, not afflicting); Mars here is Lagna lord providing drive-architecture. Suppressing them would diminish chart's authentic authority. APPROVED.

### §2.3 — Venkateshwara alignment

The Dharma Devata derivation (Aquarius = 9th from Karakamsa Gemini; Aquarius lord = Saturn; Saturn's Venkateshwara form) is classically correct. The 2025 Vishnu/Venkateshwara pivot empirically confirmed. The Codex's Venkateshwara-primary recommendation is chart-aligned. APPROVED.

### §2.4 — Potential concern: practice-density calibration

The Codex suggests a relatively full practice framework (daily mantras, weekly day-specific, Shree Sukta, Vishnu Sahasranama, etc.). For a chart whose Mercury+Moon avastha is Susupta (prefers internalization), elaborate ritual density may be architecturally excessive. The Codex acknowledges this in §1 (contemplative-jnana preferred over heavy ritualism), but Part 1 §3.1's daily mantra schedule could be read as high-density.

**Finding**: Codex is internally consistent (explicitly states dharmic-over-ritualist preference) but the reader should calibrate practice density to personal capacity, favoring consistency over intensity. Recommend adding an explicit "minimum viable practice" tier for busy periods.

### §2.5 — Approval

Remedial Codex Parts 1-2 APPROVED with minor refinement note.
